Horses, Sharks and Hearts

We carry a silent intelligence in our chests — an ever-present hum that remembers joy, grief, courage. But how often do we actually listen?

Our modern lives push us to look outward — at data, deadlines, diagnoses. We forget that our hearts store stories, sense shifts in our own energy, and whisper warnings.

Horses can pick up on a human’s heartbeat from several feet away and are known to synchronise with it in calm or distress. Their entire body listens.

Sharks, using electroreceptors called ampullae of Lorenzini, can detect the faint electric field of a beating heart — even without seeing it. It’s not about aggression. It’s about awareness.

What if we leaned into the subtle? A few breaths asking, what’s my chest saying right now? A moment of stillness to catch the tremble of emotion before our mind labels it. This is heart-healing — not fixing, but tuning. Learning that being aware is a gift.

And here, the shark’s gift to us isn’t a fearful lesson — it’s an invitation. Sharks don’t judge. They sense. They pause at the unfamiliar. What would it look like if we did the same?

In this gentle reckoning, heart-healing becomes a shared current — an echo across species — that whispers: feel first, then move.
